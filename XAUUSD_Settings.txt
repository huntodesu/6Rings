1. **Why StopLossPoints = 800?**
   - Gold trades in points (not pips)
   - 100 points = $1 move
   - 800 points = $8 stop loss
   - Typical gold daily ATR: 1500-2500 points
   - 800 pts â‰ˆ 30-50% of ATR (good buffer)

   - 2-bar confirmation reduces whipsaws
   - Still catches real trends early

   - Gold is more volatile
   - Losses can compound quickly
   - Stricter limits protect capital

   - MACD (1.5): Gold respects MACD crossovers
   - MTM (1.5): Gold is momentum-driven
   - BBI (1.4): Multi-timeframe trend important
   - RSI (0.9): Less reliable for gold
   - LWR (0.7): Redundant, lower weight

1. **POINT vs PIP Confusion**
   - XAUUSD: 1 point = $0.01 move (e.g., 2000.00 to 2000.01)
   - 100 points = $1 move
   - Your SL of 800 points = $8 move (NOT 80 pips!)

2. **Broker Differences**
   - Some brokers quote 2 decimals: 2000.45
   - Some quote 3 decimals: 2000.456
   - Check _Point value: Print(_Point) should show 0.01 or 0.001
   - Adjust StopLossPoints accordingly!

3. **Lot Size Impact**
   - Gold lot sizes vary by broker
   - Standard: 100oz (1 lot = $100/point)
   - Micro: 1oz (1 lot = $1/point)
   - Check with your broker!
   - Dynamic lots will auto-calculate correctly

4. **Spread Considerations**
   - Gold spread: typically 20-50 points during active hours
   - Can widen to 100+ during news or Asian session
   - MaxSpreadPoints = 50 prevents trading in high spread
   - Time filter helps avoid wide spreads

5. **Margin Requirements**
   - Gold requires significant margin
   - 1 mini lot (0.1) â‰ˆ $200-400 margin typically
   - Ensure sufficient margin for dynamic lot sizing
   - Account size: minimum $1,000-2,000 recommended

6. **News Impact**
   - Gold extremely sensitive to:
     * USD news (NFP, FOMC, CPI)
     * Geopolitical events
     * Risk-on/risk-off sentiment
   - Consider pausing EA during major news
   - MaxDrawdown protection helps limit damage
